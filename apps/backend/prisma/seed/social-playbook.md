# Handled Social Playbook

The knowledge Handled runs on. When any owner texts HI, the planner maps their
business to an **archetype** below and plans like a specialist in it. If no
archetype fits, the Playbook Engine researches one, appends it here, and then
plans for that customer (see `playbook-engine.md`).

- **Human-readable + machine-parseable.** Each archetype uses the exact same
  field headings so code can parse this file. Don't rename fields.
- **Living document.** Every archetype carries a `Researched` date. Anything
  older than ~6 months is stale — algorithms move — and gets re-researched.
- **Evidence-based.** Cross-cutting facts are drawn from 2025–2026 large-sample
  benchmark studies (Rival IQ, Sprout Social, Buffer, Later, Hootsuite,
  BrightLocal) and platform guidance (Mosseri/Instagram, TikTok). Sources at
  the bottom. Per-archetype tactics combine those facts with vertical practice.

## Part 1 — Cross-cutting truths (apply to every archetype)

### The 2026 algorithm reality
- **Shares/sends are the top signal now.** On Instagram, a post getting sent
  in DMs is weighted heavily — it means the content was worth spreading. Optimize
  for "who would tag a friend on this," not likes. (Mosseri's stated top Reel
  signals: watch time, likes, shares/sends.)
- **Watch time rules video.** Reels/TikTok ranking is driven by watch time,
  completion rate, replays, and reshares — above captions, hashtags, or sound.
  The **first 3 seconds** decide distribution: open on the payoff, a question,
  or motion, never a slow intro.
- **Social is a search engine.** Google now indexes public Instagram and
  short-form video, and Instagram/TikTok index keyword-rich captions, on-screen
  text, and alt text. Write captions with the words a local customer would
  search ("best birria tacos in [city]"), not just vibes. Niche community
  hashtags (#[city]eats) beat huge generic ones.
- **Original, un-watermarked, un-polished wins.** Instagram down-ranks recycled/
  watermarked content from other apps. Lo-fi phone-shot footage reads as
  authentic and outperforms glossy ads. ~1/3 of consumers are *less* likely to
  choose a brand whose ads look AI-generated — for local, real faces beat
  synthetic polish.
- **Follower count is dead as a metric.** Reach now depends on interaction
  quality (watch time, shares, saves), which levels the field — a 600-follower
  café can outreach a national chain on a single great Reel.

### Format hierarchy (local business default)
1. **Reels / short video** — reach and discovery. Non-negotiable for growth.
2. **Carousels** — highest *engagement* format on Instagram in 2025 (Rival IQ);
   great for saves, teaching, before/afters, menus.
3. **Single photo** — still useful for speed and Stories; weakest for reach.
4. **Stories** — retention and community with people who already follow you
   (polls, "we're open," behind-the-scenes), not discovery.

### Cadence (sustainable for a solo owner)
- Target **3–5 feed posts/week** + **1–2 Stories/day** (Buffer, 9.6M posts).
  Consistency beats volume; national brands post ~8–9×/day, but for a local
  owner that's a trap — quality/strategy wins now that content saturation is at
  an all-time high (Sprout). Handled's tiers ($99=3/wk, $349=7/wk) fit this.

### Best posting times (local audience, their timezone)
- Later (6M+ posts): overall best **5 AM**; **Monday** strongest day; Reels
  skew very early (~12 AM–5 AM). For nano accounts (1K–5K followers — most local
  businesses) **weekdays ~5 PM** performs well.
- Buffer (9.6M posts): **Thu 9 AM, Wed 12 PM, Wed 6 PM**; evenings 6–11 PM
  strong most weekdays; **Fri/Sat weakest**.
- **Handled's rule:** post in the customer's local timezone, biased to the hour
  *before* their audience acts — pre-commute (7–9 AM) and pre-decision for
  meals (11 AM lunch, 4–6 PM dinner). Then let per-account data override the
  benchmark after ~4 weeks.

### Where the other platforms matter
- **Google Business Profile is the highest-ROI "social" channel for local** and
  is under-used. GBP Posts, fresh photos, Q&A, and *review velocity* drive Map
  Pack ranking and "near me" discovery. Every service/foot-traffic archetype
  should post to GBP weekly and chase reviews relentlessly. (BrightLocal.)
- **Pinterest** matters for *planning-driven, visual, female-skewing* verticals:
  florists, bakeries, boutique retail, photographers, salons (bridal/color).
  It's a search engine with a long content half-life — pins drive traffic for
  months. Skip it for plumbers, gyms, vets.
- **Facebook** still matters for 35+ local audiences, events, and community
  groups (Buy-Nothing, neighborhood, "moms of [town]"). Lower engagement growth
  than IG (+9% vs +28%) but real for reach into older locals and event RSVPs.

### Metrics that actually mean revenue (track these, ignore vanity)
- **Profile visits → link/direction/call taps** ("action rate"): intent.
- **Saves + shares per post:** the modern reach engine and purchase-intent
  proxy — worth more than likes.
- **DMs started / "booking" keyword replies:** direct pipeline.
- **GBP: calls, direction requests, website clicks, new reviews:** closest thing
  to a cash register on social.
- **Reach from non-followers %:** whether you're actually growing.
- Vanity (follower count, raw likes, impressions): report lightly, never optimize.

### Universal mistakes
Being a billboard (all promo, no story) · inconsistency (2 weeks on, silence) ·
posting then ghosting (community/replies are half the algorithm now) ·
watermarked reposts · no captions/on-screen text (30% watch on mute) · chasing
trends off-brand · ignoring GBP and reviews · measuring followers not actions.

### ARCHETYPE: Axe Throwing & Social Sport Bar Playbook
- **Maps from:** axe throwing near me, axe throwing Torrance, throwing lanes, league night, bullseye, corporate team event, bachelorette party venue, walk-ins welcome, bar and games, throw night, axe throwing venue in torrance with leagues and bar, axe throwing bar, hatchet throwing, axe throwing birthday party, corporate team building activities near me, date night activities, bachelorette party ideas, things to do near me tonight.
- **Platforms:** Instagram; TikTok; Facebook; Google Business Profile.
- **Content pillars:** Bullseye and trick-shot moments; First-timer nerves to victory arc; Group celebrations (birthdays, bachelorettes, corporate); Coach/host personality and banter; Behind-the-scenes: axe maintenance, lane setup, safety briefing; League nights and leaderboard drama.
- **Top formats:** Slow-motion axe stick close-up; Group reaction shots after a throw; Leaderboard reveal graphic; Duel-style split screen (two throwers, one target); POV from behind the thrower's shoulder.
- **Cadence:** 4-5 posts per week; 1 Reel daily on weekends (Fri-Sun); Leaderboard/league update every Monday; Story polls or countdowns during slow weekday afternoons.
- **Reels that work:** Axe leaves hand, freeze-frame right before it sticks dead center; First-timer visibly terrified, then nails a bullseye on throw two; Coach blindfolded trick shot (with spotter, clearly staged for fun); Group countdown '3-2-1 THROW' synced across five lanes, all axes stick; Bachelorette party screaming reaction compilation, axes mid-flight.
- **Photo style:** High-contrast, slightly gritty warehouse lighting that makes the wood grain of the targets and the glint of the axe blade pop, shot low and close to the lane..
- **Caption hooks:** "She said she'd never held an axe before. Watch throw #3."; "This is what a perfect stick sounds like."; "Corporate team building just got a lot more honest."; "Nobody warned him about the recoil."; "League night got heated. Leaderboard below.".
- **Local discovery:** Optimize GBP for 'axe throwing near me' and '[city] axe throwing bar'; Geotag every post with venue location, not just city; Use community hashtags like #[city]datenight #[city]thingstodo; Encourage reviews mentioning 'birthday party' and 'team building' explicitly; Post in local Facebook groups for events and things-to-do roundups; Cross-tag corporate clients and bachelorette parties who book group events.
- **Offers that convert:** Bring 6 friends, one throws free; Weekday happy hour lane discount; Corporate team-building package with private coach; Bachelorette/bachelor party photo package included; First-time thrower welcome discount.
- **Seasonal hooks:** New Year 'resolution smash' promo (throw away bad habits, literally); Valentine's date night duel package; Summer outdoor league tournament; Halloween 'axe the fear' costume night; Holiday office party booking push (Nov-Dec); March Madness style bracket tournament for locals.
- **Mistakes:** Never post footage that shows unsafe throwing technique or lack of protective barriers; Always blur or crop out minors' faces unless parental consent is on file; Don't exaggerate safety certifications or 'zero injury' claims; Avoid posting alcohol consumption alongside active throwing lanes; Get verbal or written consent before featuring private parties (bachelorette, corporate) by name or company.
- **Revenue metric:** Track bookings generated from social-driven GBP calls/website clicks against average party size revenue to measure content ROI..
- **Researched:** 2026-07-20 (researched, confidence 0.80).
